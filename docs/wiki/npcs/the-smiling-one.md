## Description

Cheng Yesho—known around Willowshore as "The Smiling One"—is [Mido's](mido.md) brother and a member of the Yeshou family. He is perpetually smiling, no matter the circumstances. When misfortune befalls the oni or their allies, his expression brightens. When the new regime stumbles, he seems quietly pleased. He is always in the background, always watching, always grinning.

He is a decorated military veteran who received a **gold medal with gem inlays** for bravery beyond the call of duty during his younger years. The medal was signed by a general and describes a distinguished act of valor.

---

## Knowledge

Cheng has experience with the **Chthonic language** (the Devil's tongue) and was able to translate the term **Gosembiki** (五芒星, the Five Pointed Star) for his sister—the name [Magistrate Kurosawa](magistrate-kurosawa.md) gave to haunted ruins east of Willowshore.

He also claims to know [the true ending of the oldest story about the forest shadows](../locations/willowshore.md#the-forest-story)—a tale whose ending is never spoken aloud because it embarrasses the ruling family.

---

## Relationships

### [Mido](mido.md)
His sister and the family matriarch. Cheng serves as a trusted advisor and translator, particularly for matters involving the Chthonic language.

### The Party
When Littlefinger returned his military medal, Cheng came forward—still smiling—and took it back with obvious delight. He has not spoken directly to the party at length.

- Boone had a vivid dream of the **Battle of Five Crows**—a massacre from the old war—in which a younger Cheng was performing a chanting ritual over a circle inscribed in mud. With each repetition, his face twisted further into an unnatural grin that would never leave him. The ritual ripped open the sky and summoned a demonic entity
- When asked about the Battle of Five Crows, his eyes dilated briefly—recognition, fear, or excitement—but his permanent smile never changed; gave a shallow nod of acknowledgment

- Ginkgo caught Cheng **glancing at Mido** before answering (Perception 20)—the same kind of loaded look exchanged between Mido and Kurosawa at the festival
- Wrote two words: **"The old ones"**—referring to the five ancient gods banished by the current pantheon
- Looked directly at Ginkgo when the cleric said "the old gods" aloud
- His permanent smile is now understood to be the **physical consequence** of the battlefield ritual—his face distorted during the chanting and never recovered
