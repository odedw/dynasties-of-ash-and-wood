## Description

The Grandmother is the spirit of the [Hollow of Seven Cedars](../locations/hollow-of-seven-cedars.md) — the entity pilgrims petitioned for eons at the Lovers' Grove. Her domain is the vast **interweaving of roots** beneath the stone basin, connecting all seven cedars; when the drained basin cracked and vented in Session Twelve, the party glimpsed that root-lattice through the fissures, and something *almost* like a face in the shadows — *"like seeing an elephant in the clouds."*

Only [Ginkgo](../pcs/ginkgo.md) can hear her. Having challenged the pool and passed its test in Session Eleven, he is now the **speaker of the pool**; her voice reaches him through the mycelial groundswell — quiet, tired, wavery, elderly.

Her ancient function was **subtractive mercy**: pilgrims cast their darkest memories into her pool, and she **ate the darkness**, poisoning herself in their place so the living could heal. She has done this, by her own account, for **thousands of years**.

---

## The Binding

[Kurosawa's](magistrate-kurosawa.md) **Root Correction (the Second Key)** — the geomantic rite behind the copper nails and the grafted root — did not merely corrupt the grove. It **bound the Grandmother and inverted her**: for nearly a year she has been forced to *eat the good memories* of [Willowshore's](../locations/willowshore.md) people, funneled down the ley line, changing the town's history by changing what it remembers. She does not know *why* he wants this; she only knows what her stolen gift is being made to do.

She is **powerless to act** beyond what the ritual compels — unable to speak to anyone but the pool's speaker, unable to reach her *"allies in the woods."*

Critically, she did **not** send the [Warden](warden-of-the-grove.md). The seal corrupted the whole grove, Warden included; the same crowding *voices* Ginkgo felt puppeting the creature are the seal(s) restricting her.

---

## What She Offers

Spoken to Ginkgo across the cracked basin (Session Twelve):

- **The grove does not have to die.** If the party **reverses the Root Correction** — its steps performed in reverse order, *"by voice and by actions,"* giving back the name and undoing the seals — she can *"open my mouth again... and eat the darkness."* The originator is not required
- **What is lost is lost.** *"What went downhill must remain downhill."* The good memories already eaten from Willowshore cannot be returned; the townsfolk *"will not remember you kindly."* Reversal stops the infection; it does not cure the wound
- **After she is freed:** eyes and ears in the woods, such allies as she has — but *"no army and no hero"*
- She knows the ritual by its **shape and diagram**, not its maker; she cannot say who Kurosawa is, only what his working did

Ginkgo probed her for deception (Perception 23) and found **none** — only the same restriction, the same seal, pressing on her voice.

## Open Questions

- **Which seal actually binds her?** She has been bound *"nearly a year"* — a timeline that matches the **older erasure** over [Cassian Voss's](cassian-voss.md) name better than Kurosawa's recent nails. Kurosawa's own note admits his diagram *"alone will not open what it alone did not close"* — see [the two seals](magistrate-kurosawa.md#the-lockbox-note-the-second-seal). Will reversing *his* rite alone be enough to free her?
- **What is "the name" that must be given back?** Voss's scratched-out name is the obvious candidate — but not the confirmed one
- **What are the other presences** still crowding her grove after the Warden's death — and will they permit a reversal to be performed?
- **Is her account complete?** She is honest as far as Ginkgo can tell — but she is also bound, and *"there are barriers to my speech and what I can do."*
