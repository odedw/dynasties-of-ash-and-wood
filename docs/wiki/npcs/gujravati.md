## Description

Gujravati is a golden dragon whose form manifested in a frozen moment of time inside the collapsing [Gosembiki ruins](../locations/gosembiki-ruins.md) after the party pulled [Kurosawa's](magistrate-kurosawa.md) device free and the chasm opened. The dragon has no wings when it appears—it simply *rises*, forms, and speaks. Its presence slows time. When it is finished, the light collapses back into an orb and time resumes where it left off.

Gujravati is **the voice of the shared dream**—the same voice that [Mayor Masru](mayor-masru.md), [Radiant Willow](radiant-willow.md), and each of the party members has heard since Session Six. Boone's dream of the Battle of Five Crows carried a distorted echo of the same voice. Many in Willowshore have heard the dragon without knowing what it was.

---

## What Gujravati Has Said

> "You have heard me in fragments. Now hear me in truth. I am Gujravati. The wound is opened. I cannot close it for you—but I can mark those who still choose to stand against the falling darkness."

> "I am not your master. I am not your shield. I am only one who saw the rot spreading, and chose to answer before silence did."

> "What is open now will call many things to the surface—some buried, some invited, some long denied. Remember this: this wound in the land is not the first wound. Only the freshest."

---

## The Marks

In the frozen moment, Gujravati addressed each party member individually and gave them a **personal mark or gift**. The specifics of each gift were distributed as private handouts and are known only to the recipient and the GM. Collectively, they are meant as tools *"against the falling darkness."*

- Gifts are personal, not shared
- Their cost or conditions are not yet fully known
- Each bears some relationship to the recipient's nature or needs

---

## Agenda & Unknowns

Gujravati has **not** claimed to be a patron or deity. It has explicitly rejected the role of master and shield. What it wants from the party beyond resistance to "the falling darkness" is unknown.

Open questions:

- What exactly is "the falling darkness"?
- Why did Gujravati need the party to *pull the device* before it could intervene?
- Are the older wounds it referenced separate threats—or part of the same long slow disaster?
- What does invoking the marks cost? Are there limits?
- Is Gujravati still watching? Can the party reach it, or only be reached?
