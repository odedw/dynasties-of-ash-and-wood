*The horned thing that the grove keeps for the day someone comes to harm it.*

## Description

A large, **horned** creature that tore its way out of the treeline *outside* the cedar ring the instant [Boone](../pcs/boone.md) ripped the corrupting master graft from the basin floor. Seen up close (Session Twelve): **seven and a half to eight feet tall**, long-armed and lanky, with long thorned claws — a lean *swamp thing* of bark, branches, vines, and green skin, wearing no armor because its hide **is** armor. It moved through brush and trees as if they weren't there, supernaturally smooth, and fought with a *"deep fiery ire"* in its eyes.

It had **no organs, no blood, no language** — it groaned and yelled but never spoke — and blades mostly glanced off its barky exterior. **Fire** was the only thing that truly hurt it.

---

## The Puppet

The Warden was not its own master. When [Ginkgo](../pcs/ginkgo.md) reached out mid-combat — voice, open hands, mycelial feelers — he broke through for one long moment: the red rage in its eyes **softened into fear**, its angular face slackening — and then something *squeezed*, and the mask of anger snapped back on.

Ginkgo's follow-up ecology read named the mechanism: the grove is crowded with **multiple presences** — *"like a city where you expect a few voices and there are several, chattering"* — **two, maybe three** unnatural forces, slightly opposed to one another, all pulling on the creature at once. Something was *wearing* the Warden. The [Grandmother](the-grandmother.md) later confirmed she did **not** send it: the seal corrupted the whole grove, its protector included.

When it died, **the presences did not leave.** Ginkgo felt them still hanging in the grove after the leaves settled.

---

## The Death

The party genuinely tried to take it alive — Ginkgo stacked **Calm** and **Enfeeble** on it and pleaded to subdue it (*"I really want to submit this guy so we can talk to him"*); [Boone](../pcs/boone.md) announced *"We don't want to hurt you — talk to the mushroom"* while tripping it prone; [Donkey's](../pcs/donkey.md) **Tangle Vine** pinned it. But it never stopped coming. [Da Baishan](../pcs/da-baishan.md) ended it with a **Devise a Stratagem** strike keyed to his standing investigation of the pool — the blade going all the way through.

Its reaching hands closed, the green and the anger drained out of it, and it **wilted like a flower in the sun**, vines dangling limp from the polearm's blade, leaves drifting down in the sudden quiet. Da Baishan apologized to Ginkgo afterward. *"We did our best,"* Ginkgo said.

Its death — or perhaps the party's victory over it — was followed within moments by the basin **cracking open** and the [Grandmother](the-grandmother.md) speaking for the first time.

## Open Questions

- **What were the presences pulling on it** — two, maybe three, mutually opposed — and what will they do now that their puppet is dead?
- **Was there a person in there?** The moment of fear Ginkgo saw suggests something aware was trapped under the compulsion. Could it have been saved, if the fight had gone differently?
- **Will the grove raise another Warden** — before or after the party attempts the ritual reversal?
