## Description

A large, **horned** creature that tore its way out of the treeline *outside* the cedar ring the instant [Boone](../pcs/boone.md) ripped the corrupting master graft from the basin floor. It rose with the trees swaying around it and **howled into the sky.** [Da Baishan's](../pcs/da-baishan.md) Occultism check named it for what it is: a **Warden of the Grove**, the protector bound to the [Hollow of Seven Cedars](../locations/hollow-of-seven-cedars.md). It looked, unambiguously, **angry.**

It did not attack before the session cut off — its arrival *was* the cliffhanger.

---

## The Dilemma It Represents

The Warden is a clean, cruel piece of dramatic irony. The party came to the grove to **save** it — and the only known way to stop Kurosawa's corruption is to **kill all seven cedars.** The Warden exists to **protect those cedars.** Its goal and the party's goal are aimed at the same trees from opposite directions:

- If the party are right that the grove must die, the Warden will fight to stop the only available cure
- If the Warden can be made to understand the corruption, the grove's death may be the **mercy** it cannot bring itself to choose
- Whether it distinguishes between *Kurosawa's* harm and the *party's* — both of which end in dead cedars — is unknown

[Donkey](../pcs/donkey.md) (in absentia) and Boone both floated diplomacy at session's end (*"maybe it knows we're on the same side"*); [Littlefinger](../pcs/littlefinger.md) noted, dryly, that ripping a screaming root out of the grove's heart "didn't look very diplomatic."

## Open Questions

- **Is it the Dream Eater / Grandmother itself**, roused and warped — or a separate guardian that the spirit (or the grove) keeps?
- **Was it sleeping until the graft came out**, or did Kurosawa's corruption suppress it and the party freed it?
- **Can it be reasoned with**, or only fought?
- **Does it know what Kurosawa did**, or does it simply see intruders standing over a torn-out root in a drained, desecrated basin?
