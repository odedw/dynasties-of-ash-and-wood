## Description

Captain Akoto is one of the red-skinned oni guards serving under [Magistrate Kurosawa](magistrate-kurosawa.md). He patrols the streets of [Willowshore](../locations/willowshore.md) enforcing martial law. Unlike the magistrate, Akoto appears to be a straightforward soldier—he follows orders without relish and seems somewhat reasonable when presented with legitimate authority.

---

## Personality

Akoto is professional and measured. When the party presented their investigative licenses from [Mayor Masru](mayor-masru.md), he was confused but compliant. He did not enjoy the confrontation and moved on without incident. He was willing to share basic information when asked directly, admitting that the troops were told very little about the investigation.

## Session History

### Session Six (2026-03-19)
- Confronted the party on the street during martial law; ordered them back to their homes
- Read the party's investigative licenses from [Mayor Masru](mayor-masru.md); looked confused but let them pass
- When Ginkgo demanded his name and rank, he paused but complied: *"Captain Akoto"*
- Admitted the troops were told very little by [Kurosawa](magistrate-kurosawa.md)—only to enforce the lockdown and ensure no one leaves town
- Confirmed Kurosawa was first on the scene at the estate and insisted on handling the investigation alone
- Said he guessed the lockdown related to the commotion at the estate; expressed mild doubt: *"I don't think Kurosawa will be as calm as I am"*

### Session Eight (2026-04-22)
- Present during the demon attack on Willowshore; engaged one of the creatures alongside his fellow oni guards and townsfolk before the party arrived
- Called as a **prosecution witness** during the Rite of Open Reckoning
- Testified that he encountered the party leaving town toward the eastern ruins, and that they had stopped at the graveyard first *"conversing in secret—possibly with the dead"*
- Did **not** mention that Donkey had put him to Sleep with Conceal Spell during martial law (either from omission, tact, or not knowing)
- Concluded that the party *"acted on their own authority, sought no counsel, and reported to no one"*
- Professional and measured in tone; offered no personal animosity, but delivered testimony that supported Kurosawa's framing of the party as unsanctioned operators
