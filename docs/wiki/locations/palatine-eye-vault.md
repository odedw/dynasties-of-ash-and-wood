# Palatine Eye Vault

*A cliff with no door. A puzzle of murals, scrolls, and a lantern that lies. A book that only its keeper can read.*

---

## Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | The Palatine Eye Vault (Hall of Divided Testimony) |
| **Type** | Sealed occult chamber of the [Order of the Palatine Eye](../factions/order-of-the-palatine-eye.md) |
| **Location** | Cliffside in the mountains, ~40–50 miles **south** of [Willowshore](willowshore.md); ~15 miles south of [Glutton's Bridge](#approach-glutton-s-bridge) |

## Approach: Glutton's Bridge

The path south winds through cool mountain forest — pine, deciduous green, the last of the cherry-blossom trees losing petals in the wind. The trail descends into a small ravine spanned by a 10–12 foot stone footbridge, with no water below. The bridge is old, mortared stone, and someone built it long enough ago that the design is no longer in current use.

A **glutton ogre** — roughly 9 feet tall, two-legged, ambush-disciplined enough to *blend* against the rocks — has claimed the southern end of the bridge as a hunting ground. The party watched it run down a passing elk by hand. They did not engage; they distracted it with a **bamboo trebuchet** flinging a shuriken-loaded cured ham, and crossed while it was busy.

---

## The Cliffside Door

About 15 miles south of the bridge, after a couple of switchbacks at altitude, the path runs straight into a sheer rock cliff. There is no door, no carving, no gate. Only [Da Baishan](../pcs/da-baishan.md) — carrying [Vujravati's](../npcs/vujravati.md) location-mark — feels the cliff's pull, like being inside the field of a magnet.

The door opens for a *single* sequence:

1. **The hand that doubts knocks.** [Donkey's](../pcs/donkey.md) reasoning — *"my hand doubts that knocking will work, so it is the doubting hand"* — got him through the threshold of the riddle and to a knock; but the cliff was solid stone for him.
2. **Da Baishan's knock answered.** Two raps on the same stone produced a different sound — metallic, deeper than rock — and a closed eye traced itself in the cliff face. The same eye drew itself, simultaneously, in henna-style ink on the back of Da Baishan's hand.
3. **The eye opens.** When the figure completed, the cliff opened along the line of the eye, revealing a 30-step stone staircase descending ~20 feet into a sealed circular chamber.

The eye on Da Baishan's hand opened with the door, and stayed open as long as the door stood open. **Da Baishan recalled, like a struck bell, that [Cassian Voss](../npcs/cassian-voss.md) had this same mark on the back of his hand.**

## The Hall of Divided Testimony

The first chamber is a **20-foot circular room** with a panoramic mural of pen-and-ink scenes painted continuously around the curved wall. The far side is a **stone door with four bronze dials** and an inscription:

> *"Lies do not bury truth. They show where truth was wounded."*

The dials are labeled **Moon**, **Willow**, **Bridge**, and **Flame.** A **jade dragon-coil ring** at the center of the dial array locks the answer when turned.

In the ceiling: a **black metal lantern** with a steady blue flame that gives off **no heat**. On the floor: **four short scrolls,** like fortune-cookie strips, named for elements of the puzzle.

### The trick

Each of the four scrolls reads as a literal hint to the dial it shares a theme with. **Each scroll is also lying.** The mural shows where each lie is wounded.

The lantern is the key — but only when the scrolls are **read aloud to it.** When read into the lantern's light, the cast shadows lengthen across the floor, reach the mural, and **reshape the painted scene** to show the truth that the spoken lie revealed:

| Scroll read aloud | Mural answers | Dial set to |
|---|---|---|
| *Pale Witness* (Moon) | Moon shifts from the *first arrival* tower to the **opposite** tower (the one nearest the sealed door) | **Right tower** |
| *Weeping Root* (Willow) | A trail of black leaves climbs from the courtyard, past the grave marker, up to the shrine roof; the willow's branches hang from the roof like prayer cords | **Shrine roof** |
| *Loyal Crossing* (Bridge) | The left and right arches walk away as painted soldiers; only the center arch remains | **One arch** |
| *Devout Flame* (Flame) | The kneeling monk stands, raises his **left** hand palm-out, swears a vow with a flame above his head; the eye appears on the back of that left hand as he lowers it | **Monk's left hand** |

The Order's solution is therefore: **Right tower / Shrine roof / One arch / Monk's left hand.** The dragon-ring locked, the door ground 10 feet back into the wall, and a 30-foot ink-dark hallway formed from the ceiling pouring down — revealing the inner sanctum.

> *"Lies do not bury truth. They show where truth was wounded."*

## The Sanctum and the Stone Witness

The inner chamber is another circular room, ~20 ft across. In the center: a **kneeling stone figure**, ~12 feet tall when standing, with **no mouth and a single closed eye** in its face. It is not standing guard — it is in the posture of a witness called and waiting.

Above and behind the figure, in the same script as the outer hall:

> *"The eye does not open for strength. It opens for the hand that accepts being seen."*

When [Boone](../pcs/boone.md) approached with his **eye-marked hand raised** (the same mark Da Baishan had taken from the cliff knock), the statue stood, opened its single eye, raised the same hand, and walked, robotic, to the wall — placing its palm under the inscription. A small **alcove** opened beneath, holding a **lectern** and a single book.

The statue did not move again. It is still standing inside the sealed cliffside, in the open posture, watching the door.

---

## The Book of Divided Testimony

A leather-bound mage-style tome, locked by a strap and a mechanical clasp with no keyhole. The cover reads, **The Book of Divided Testimony.**

To the rest of the party, the book is **blank.** Every page Boone turned was empty paper. To **Boone alone** the pages held words.

The eye-tattoo on the back of his hand grew warm against the lock; the latch released to him directly. The first page is the Order's catechism:

> *"Truth does not rescue. Truth is responsibility."*

The book is offered as a class-style **dedication into the Order** — Boone may take the **Palatine Detective** archetype after reading it. Beyond the dedication pages, the book contains *fragments* of testimony from past members of the Order, available as the bearer earns them. Boone rolled twice on a 1d8 random table; **two of eight possible truths** revealed themselves to him on first reading. *Six remain.*

### Truth I — Mido's Half-Truth

> *Beware the elder who lies by subtraction.*
>
> *She does not hide the road because she serves the dark.*
>
> *She hides the road because she knows what followed the last traveler home.*
>
> *A tyrant believes law can replace loyalty.*
>
> *A geomancer believes lines can replace roots.*

This entry concerns **[Mido](../npcs/mido.md).** It does not accuse her of treachery; it accuses her of *omission*. It identifies her secrecy not as collaboration but as fear of provoking the same outcome again — *"what followed the last traveler home."* The party does not yet know who that traveler was.

### Truth II — Kurosawa's Error

> *A tyrant believes law can replace loyalty.*
>
> *A geomancer believes lines can replace roots.*
>
> *Both are wrong until the roots are made to forget themselves.*

This entry names **[Magistrate Kurosawa's](../npcs/magistrate-kurosawa.md)** error, and gives the party — for the first time — the *theoretical objection* to his ritual. Kurosawa's plan is conditional. The Mother of a Thousand Wings cannot be invoked through ley-line geomancy alone. **The roots themselves must be made to forget what they are.** That is exactly the function of the [Hollow of Seven Cedars](hollow-of-seven-cedars.md), the Root key, and exactly the role of its **Dream Eater** in his rite.

The mismatch between Kurosawa's theory and his actual plan is the seam in his confidence the Order means the party to find. If the Hollow's spirit refuses to forget, the entire Confluence fails.

---

## Sealing

The party left the sanctum carrying the book. The eye on the cliff face closed silently behind them. The cliff is now smooth stone again, with no visible seam. Only the eye-tattoo on the back of [Boone's](../pcs/boone.md) hand — and his copy of the **Book of Divided Testimony** — would let them back in.

[Da Baishan](../pcs/da-baishan.md) chose to drop his cliff-mark tattoo on the way out, ceding the role of the Order's named bearer to Boone, who was the one the book had answered.

## Open Questions

- Why was [Cassian Voss](../npcs/cassian-voss.md) marked with the same eye? Was he Order? An associate? A failed candidate?
- Are the other **six** truths in the book person-named? Place-named? Tied to the keys?
- The lantern, the murals, the stone witness — none decayed. Are these actively maintained, or held in stasis?
- The cliff sealed when the party left. **Does it open for any other party member, ever, now that Da Baishan dropped the mark and Boone took the book?**
