## Description

The Hollow of Seven Cedars is an ancient and rarely visited place west of [Willowshore](willowshore.md). Seven enormous cedars — redwood-thick, eons old — stand in a near-perfect ring, their high branches woven into a closed canopy. At the heart of the ring is an almost perfectly round stone basin, like an oversized bird-bath, ringed by ivory-white alabaster stones. In older days, when more people knew the road, it was called simply **Lovers' Grove**.

The site is held to be the home of a **dream spirit** — sometimes called the *Dream Eater*, or the **Grandmother**. Tradition holds that the spirit's gender is unknown and unimportant; it accepts what is given to it and asks nothing else.

### As the party found it (Session Eleven)

The grove was *wrong* long before the party reached the ring. On the approach trail they found a **cut red ribbon** and a broken child's toy — grave-tokens that belonged *at* the grove, somehow strewn *away* from it. The forest itself was healthy (no wounded-land sickness like the [Dark Woods](dark-woods.md)), but [Ginkgo](../pcs/ginkgo.md) felt a *dissociation* in the air, and an [Augury](../pcs/ginkgo.md) confirmed something was deeply off.

Inside the ring:

- The white alabaster stones were **blackened and marbled**, marred where they had been unblemished
- The central pool was **inky, light-swallowing black** — no reflection, no shimmer
- Heavy **copper nails** — railroad-tie thick, sledge-driven — were hammered into every cedar, **bark-side facing the basin**, wounding the trees. Black, inky sap bled from each wound, exactly like the corruption at the [Mourndusk Willow](dark-woods.md)
- On the **third cedar**, ~20 inches off the ground, frantic knife-scratches had **gouged out a carved name**, leaving only the letters **·S·S·I·N·V·O·S·S** — [Cassian Voss](../npcs/cassian-voss.md). In the gouges: a fine **khaki/mustard powder**, identified as finely-ground **moth-wing carapace** — the same signature as the moths that killed the lanterns at the mayor's inauguration and the wings found at the [willow](dark-woods.md)

## The Grandmother / Dream Eater

The grove's spirit went by several names — most often the **Grandmother** or the **Dream Eater**. The legend [Littlefinger](../pcs/littlefinger.md) and Donkey recalled (Society 23) holds that if she *answered your plea*, she would **eat the dark memory** you brought her — the grief, the guilt, the thing you could not carry — and let you keep the good. The eaten memory was said to **poison her** in your place; that was the bargain. Pilgrims always brought a **memento** (a ribbon, a soldier's medal, a dead child's toy) as the anchor of the thing to be removed.

The function was fundamentally **subtractive and protective**: she took the worst of what people remembered so the rest could heal. Kurosawa's corruption **inverts** exactly this — see below.

---

## The Old Practice

Pilgrims came to the Hollow to **cast off** the dreams and desires they wished to be free of, and to **keep** the ones they cherished.

The rite, as [Mido](../npcs/mido.md) recalls it:

1. Bring a **trinket** that anchors the unwanted dream — a child's toy, a soldier's badge, a lover's token, an emblem of war
2. Place the trinket inside one of the **seven cedars** (the trees have been worn smooth by hands and grief)
3. Speak the dream or memory into the **pool** at the heart of the grove
4. Walk away changed — relieved of the harshest edge of the thing remembered, but not of the memory itself

People came here for many reasons:

- A spouse with a wandering eye, asking for the desire to be removed
- A grieving parent, asking to keep love for a dead child but not the daily, gnawing pain
- Veterans of old wars, depositing badges and dog-tags they could no longer carry
- Lovers who wished to forget another lover, or to remember only the good of a parted one

The place has long been considered private and sacred — even those who do not believe in spirits avert their eyes when passing nearby.

## Significance to Kurosawa's Plan

[Kurosawa's](../npcs/magistrate-kurosawa.md) recovered note (the **Note of Five Keys**) names the Hollow as the **second** of his five ley-line targets. He labels it the **Root** key:

> *"The old lovers' grove, now called the Hollow of Seven Cedars, must be opened at the root next. Memory can be cut, grafted, and made to grow in a new direction."*

[Mido](../npcs/mido.md) interprets this as Kurosawa intending to **invert** the spirit's old function — instead of helping people *cast off* unwanted memory, the Dream Eater would be made to *prune and graft* memory in the townspeople of [Willowshore](willowshore.md). What the survivors would forget — and what they would remember instead — would be Kurosawa's choice.

> *"Will the surviving people forget their loved ones? Will they remember only the dark thoughts? I'm not exactly sure what he wants with it, but it seems very important to his next phase."*
> — [Mido](../npcs/mido.md)

If the legends are true and the spirit still exists, it is presumably still bound to the cedars and the pool by the same agreement that held it there for centuries.

## Kurosawa's Rite (the vision in the pool)

When [Da Baishan](../pcs/da-baishan.md) touched the black water — drawn by the eye-mark warming on the back of his hand — the basin showed him, fisheye-from-below, **what was done here.** It was the first time the party has *watched* [Kurosawa](../npcs/magistrate-kurosawa.md) perform a key ritual:

- Kurosawa knelt alone at the basin under recent moonlight, hands black with wet soil, a sledge-mallet and a pouch of long copper nails at his side
- He held a **severed, blackened root** about a foot and a half long, which **squirmed hungrily** toward the water as he chanted
- He spoke an incantation — the line he *mumbled*, that the vision carried up to the party:

> *"Memory is not sacred. It is only precedent. And precedent can be overturned."*

- He pressed the root into the water; its inky coating bled off into the pool; it **grafted itself onto one of the great cedar roots** in the basin floor and the blackness streaked outward through all seven interconnected root systems

The vision then **cut to Willowshore** to show the *purpose*: husbands remembering only disappointment, children remembering only fear, neighbors forgetting debts of kindness. [Liwen](../npcs/liwen.md) looking at the party without recognition. [Radiant Willow](../npcs/radiant-willow.md) lowering a hand she had once raised for them — *no longer able to remember why she trusted them.* [Yong](../npcs/yong.md) keeping the memory of his rescued son, but fixating instead on the belief that **the party caused the danger in the first place.** The grove's old gift — *eat the dark, keep the good* — turned exactly inside out, the corruption flowing from the Seven Cedars **into the town.**

This is the Root key in operation: not a weapon, but a **machine for poisoning a town's memory of itself.**

## The Verdict: The Trees Must Die

The vision left Da Baishan with one final, flat truth, and [Ginkgo's](../pcs/ginkgo.md) own consultation with the **mycelium** (Nature/ecology 21) confirmed it:

- The poison is in **all seven cedars' interconnected roots**, not just the grafted one
- Pulling the nails is **not enough**. Draining the basin is **not enough**. Tearing out the master graft is **not enough**
- There is **no known cure and no extraction** — this is *arcane/occult corruption*, not a natural blight or poison that can be salved
- The only paths even hypothesized: **knowing the originating ritual** (to reverse it), or going *back before it was implemented*
- **To stop the corruption, the grove itself — all seven ancient cedars — must die.** Killing the grove also presumably ends the Dream Eater's gift forever

Ginkgo, a leshy, found this almost impossible to accept (*"these are old trees, older than all of us"*) — and then jumped into the pool anyway to try.

## The Pool's Second Vision (Ginkgo)

When Ginkgo leapt in, the basin showed *him* a different scene — a test rather than a record: two childhood friends laughing on a bench; one called to war and one not; they swear they will never be enemies. Seasons blur; a battlefield; two soldiers duel; the victor lifts the slain one's helmet and it is the friend who went to war. The water asked:

> *"Who do you choose? Even when there is no blood law or prophet bind — who would you save?"*

Ginkgo answered: his **childhood friend from the village he left behind.** The vision honored the answer, and the water **drained into the earth**, leaving him at the bottom of the dry basin beside the throbbing, heartbeat-alive master graft.

## The Warden of the Grove

The instant [Boone](../pcs/boone.md) ripped the master graft free (Athletics 25) — black ichor splattering, the root writhing for fifteen seconds before going limp and ashen — the trees *outside* the ring swayed and a **horned creature tore out of the treeline, howling at the sky.** Da Baishan's Occultism identified it: a **[Warden of the Grove](../npcs/warden-of-the-grove.md)**, the grove's protector, and it looked **angry.**

In Session Twelve the party fought it — trying and failing to subdue it — and killed it. It proved to be a **puppet**: [Ginkgo's](../pcs/ginkgo.md) mid-combat ecology read found **two, maybe three opposed, unnatural presences** crowding the grove and pulling on the creature, *"like a city of chattering voices."* Those presences **survived its death** and still linger in the Hollow.

---

## The Grandmother Unbound — Almost (Session Twelve)

After the Warden fell, the drained basin **cracked and vented** — an exhale of air and mist — revealing through the fissures the vast **root-lattice interconnecting all seven cedars.** From beneath it, the **[Grandmother](../npcs/the-grandmother.md)** spoke to Ginkgo (and only Ginkgo — as the pool's speaker):

- She has been **bound nearly a year**, inverted, forced to eat Willowshore's *good* memories down the ley line
- **The grove does not have to die.** Reverse the binding rite — the **Root Correction, the Second Key**, Kurosawa's custom geomantic ritual — and she can *"open my mouth again... and eat the darkness,"* stopping any further corruption
- The reversal requires the ritual's **steps performed in reverse order, by voice and by actions** — *"give back the name and undo the seals."* The **originator is not required**
- **What's already lost stays lost**: *"what went downhill must remain downhill."* Willowshore's eaten memories cannot be restored

The party stole the ritual — the only copy, in Kurosawa's own hand — from his estate chambers that night, along with **five spare copper nails** and [his lockbox note](../npcs/magistrate-kurosawa.md#the-lockbox-note-the-second-seal).

## The Two Seals

The stolen papers, analyzed at camp against everything the party knows, revealed that the Hollow carries **two distinct seals from two different hands**:

| | Seal One — the Root Correction | Seal Two — the Erasure |
|---|---|---|
| **Hand** | [Kurosawa](../npcs/magistrate-kurosawa.md) — confirmed by his own papers | **Unknown** — explicitly *not* Kurosawa; he is investigating it himself |
| **Character** | Orderly geomantic lines, diagrams, *transformation* | Frantic knife-work — *"haste, emotion, emotional removal"* |
| **Physical trace** | The copper nails, the grafted root, the inverted pool | The scratched-out [Cassian Voss](../npcs/cassian-voss.md) name, packed with **moth-wing powder** |
| **Age** | Recent — within months of the party's arrival | **Older** — 6 months to a year; before Kurosawa's nails |
| **Effect** | Inverted the Grandmother's gift; aimed it at Willowshore | *"Shut the mouth"* of the grove — an undoing that Kurosawa found already in place and built on top of |

Kurosawa's note is blunt about the dependency: *"My nails hold on top of another hand's work. Useful. But it means my diagram alone will not open what it alone did not close."* The party's open worry is the mirror image: whether reversing **his** ritual alone can free the Grandmother from a binding that partially predates him. Her *"nearly a year"* timeline matches the **erasure**, not the nails.

## Approach

Roughly 10 miles west of [Willowshore](willowshore.md). The trail passes through scattered woods that thicken as the road descends toward the grove. The cedars are visible from a distance — seven dark spires above the canopy, taller than the surrounding trees, set in a near-perfect ring.

## Open Questions

- **Will the reversal work?** The party holds the Root Correction and the Grandmother's instructions — but Kurosawa's own note warns his diagram sits *on top of* a seal it did not create. Does the reversal need to address **both seals**?
- **What is "the name" that must be given back?** [Voss's](../npcs/cassian-voss.md) scratched-out name is the obvious candidate, but unconfirmed
- **What are the lingering presences?** Two or three, mutually opposed, still in the grove after the [Warden's](../npcs/warden-of-the-grove.md) death. Will they resist a ritual performed in their midst — and will the grove raise another Warden?
- **Whose is the second seal?** Voss's own work, the work of whatever killed him, or *"some ancient power"*? Kurosawa himself doesn't know
- **What killed [Cassian Voss](../npcs/cassian-voss.md) here**, and whose handwriting did Littlefinger and Donkey each half-recognize?
