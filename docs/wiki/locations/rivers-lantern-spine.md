## What is Known

The River's Lantern Spine is named in [Kurosawa's](../npcs/magistrate-kurosawa.md) planning note as the **Water** key. The full passage:

> *"The drowned markers of the River's Lantern Spine must be turned with the current. Water carries every name."*

The language strongly implies a series of **submerged stone or wooden markers** ("drowned markers") set in a riverbed in a line — a "spine" of lantern stones, perhaps once lit ceremonially, now swallowed by the river. *"Turned with the current"* suggests the markers must be physically realigned to flow *with* the water, not against it — reversing or redirecting whatever they were originally set to do.

The maxim *"Water carries every name"* echoes both Tian Xia funeral language (rivers carry the dead's names downstream to the sea) and Kurosawa's broader interest in **renaming** Willowshore — softening its history so that no name fixes in place except the one he chooses.

---

## Speculation

Willowshore sits at a confluence of rivers, and [Mido](../npcs/mido.md) has long held that the **five surface rivers mirror the five ley lines beneath the land**. The River's Lantern Spine may run along one of those rivers — possibly even one in or very near Willowshore itself — though the exact location is unknown.

## Open Questions

- **Which river?** Yanzy and its tributaries each could host such a line
- **What did the markers originally do?** Lights for spirits, way-stones for funerals, boundary markers between domains
- **Who tends them now?** No one the party has yet met has spoken of them by name
- **Is "turned with the current" a mechanical action, a ritual one, or both?**
