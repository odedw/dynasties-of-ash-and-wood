    Vujravati¶

    "I am not your master. I am not your shield. I am only one who saw the rot spreading, and chose to answer before silence did."

    Overview¶
    Name Vujravati
    Type Golden dragon
    Status Active — speaks in dreams, appears in visions
    Alignment (provisional) Aligned with the party against "the falling darkness"; goals beyond that unknown

    Description¶

    Vujravati is a golden dragon whose form manifested in a frozen moment of time inside the collapsing Gosembiki ruins after the party pulled Kurosawa's device free and the chasm opened. The dragon has no wings when it appears—it simply rises, forms, and speaks. Its presence slows time. When it is finished, the light collapses back into an orb and time resumes where it left off.

    Vujravati is the voice of the shared dream—the same voice that Mayor Masru, Radiant Willow, and each of the party members has heard since Session Six. Boone's dream of the Battle of Five Crows carried a distorted echo of the same voice. Many in Willowshore have heard the dragon without knowing what it was.

    What Vujravati Has Said¶

    "You have heard me in fragments. Now hear me in truth. I am Vujravati. The wound is opened. I cannot close it for you—but I can mark those who still choose to stand against the falling darkness."

    "I am not your master. I am not your shield. I am only one who saw the rot spreading, and chose to answer before silence did."

    "What is open now will call many things to the surface—some buried, some invited, some long denied. Remember this: this wound in the land is not the first wound. Only the freshest."

    The Marks¶

    In the frozen moment, Vujravati addressed each party member individually and gave them a personal mark or gift. The specifics of each gift were distributed as private handouts and are known only to the recipient and the GM. Collectively, they are meant as tools "against the falling darkness."

    • Gifts are personal, not shared
    • Their cost or conditions are not yet fully known
    • Each bears some relationship to the recipient's nature or needs

    Gifts in play¶

    • Ginkgo's gift — described in play as "a scale" worn or carried on his person; not recognized as a weapon by Kurosawa's guards when they searched the party. Includes the ability to stabilize a dying ally once per day without spell or ritual; first invoked to save Boone on the wall above Willowshore in Session Nine.
    • Other PCs' gifts remain undisclosed in-fiction.

    Agenda & Unknowns¶

    Vujravati has not claimed to be a patron or deity. It has explicitly rejected the role of master and shield. What it wants from the party beyond resistance to "the falling darkness" is unknown.

    Open questions:

    • What exactly is "the falling darkness"?
    • Why did Vujravati need the party to pull the device before it could intervene?
    • Are the older wounds it referenced separate threats—or part of the same long slow disaster?
    • What does invoking the marks cost? Are there limits?
    • Is Vujravati still watching? Can the party reach it, or only be reached?
    • Does Vujravati's "I saw the rot spreading, and chose to answer" align with the lost Order of the Palatine Eye, an extinct occult investigator order that Da Baishan's mark points him toward?

    Session History¶

    Session Six (2026-03-19)¶

    • The shared dream carries Vujravati's voice, though its source is unknown at the time; experienced by the party, Mayor Masru, Radiant Willow, and others in Willowshore
    • Boone's separate dream of the Battle of Five Crows carries a distorted echo of the same voice ("five must fall before the world is born anew")

    Session Eight (2026-04-22)¶

    • First direct appearance. Manifested in frozen time as the Gosembiki ruins collapsed around the party
    • Named itself: "I am Vujravati."
    • Revealed itself as the voice behind the shared dream
    • Gave each PC a personal mark/gift against "the falling darkness"
    • Warned that the opened wound is not the first—older wounds exist in the land
    • Dissolved back into an orb of light; time resumed

    Session Nine (2026-05-07)¶

    • First gift invoked in play: Ginkgo used Vujravati's once-per-day stabilize on Boone atop Kurosawa's earthen wall, pulling him back from death after a critical nodachi strike
    • Ginkgo's scale was searched and not recognized as a weapon by Kurosawa's guards—it stayed with him through arrest and confinement
    • Da Baishan's Occultism (21) on his mark identified it as a token of the Order of the Palatine Eye—an extinct legendary occult-investigator order; the gift carries an ingrained location ~40–50 miles south of Willowshore in the mountains
