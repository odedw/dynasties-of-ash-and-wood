    The Grandmother¶

    "I've been eating the dark for thousands of years."

    Overview¶
    Name Unknown — called the Grandmother, the Memory Eater of the Seven Cedars, the Dream Eater
    Type Ancient grove spirit (occult); gender traditionally held unknown and unimportant
    Location Beneath the basin of the Hollow of Seven Cedars, in the interwoven root system of all seven trees
    Status Alive — and bound. Sealed for nearly a year; can speak only to Ginkgo, the pool's speaker

    Description¶

    The Grandmother is the spirit of the Hollow of Seven Cedars — the entity pilgrims petitioned for eons at the Lovers' Grove. Her domain is the vast interweaving of roots beneath the stone basin, connecting all seven cedars; when the drained basin cracked and vented in Session Twelve, the party glimpsed that root-lattice through the fissures, and something almost like a face in the shadows — "like seeing an elephant in the clouds."

    Only Ginkgo can hear her. Having challenged the pool and passed its test in Session Eleven, he is now the speaker of the pool; her voice reaches him through the mycelial groundswell — quiet, tired, wavery, elderly.

    Her ancient function was subtractive mercy: pilgrims cast their darkest memories into her pool, and she ate the darkness, poisoning herself in their place so the living could heal. She has done this, by her own account, for thousands of years.

    The Binding¶

    Kurosawa's Root Correction (the Second Key) — the geomantic rite behind the copper nails and the grafted root — did not merely corrupt the grove. It bound the Grandmother and inverted her: for nearly a year she has been forced to eat the good memories of Willowshore's people, funneled down the ley line, changing the town's history by changing what it remembers. She does not know why he wants this; she only knows what her stolen gift is being made to do.

    She is powerless to act beyond what the ritual compels — unable to speak to anyone but the pool's speaker, unable to reach her "allies in the woods."

    Critically, she did not send the Warden. The seal corrupted the whole grove, Warden included; the same crowding voices Ginkgo felt puppeting the creature are the seal(s) restricting her.

    What She Offers¶

    Spoken to Ginkgo across the cracked basin (Session Twelve):

    • The grove does not have to die. If the party reverses the Root Correction — its steps performed in reverse order, "by voice and by actions," giving back the name and undoing the seals — she can "open my mouth again... and eat the darkness." The originator is not required
    • What is lost is lost. "What went downhill must remain downhill." The good memories already eaten from Willowshore cannot be returned; the townsfolk "will not remember you kindly." Reversal stops the infection; it does not cure the wound
    • After she is freed: eyes and ears in the woods, such allies as she has — but "no army and no hero"
    • She knows the ritual by its shape and diagram, not its maker; she cannot say who Kurosawa is, only what his working did

    Ginkgo probed her for deception (Perception 23) and found none — only the same restriction, the same seal, pressing on her voice.

    Open Questions¶

    • Which seal actually binds her? She has been bound "nearly a year" — a timeline that matches the older erasure over Cassian Voss's name better than Kurosawa's recent nails. Kurosawa's own note admits his diagram "alone will not open what it alone did not close" — see the two seals. Will reversing his rite alone be enough to free her?
    • What is "the name" that must be given back? Voss's scratched-out name is the obvious candidate — but not the confirmed one
    • What are the other presences still crowding her grove after the Warden's death — and will they permit a reversal to be performed?
    • Is her account complete? She is honest as far as Ginkgo can tell — but she is also bound, and "there are barriers to my speech and what I can do."

    Session History¶

    Session Eleven (2026-06-04)¶

    • Her legend recovered (Littlefinger's Society 23): the Dream Eater who ate pilgrims' dark memories and poisoned herself in their place
    • Her pool tested Ginkgo ("who would you save?"); his answer drained the basin and made him the pool's speaker

    Session Twelve (2026-07-16)¶

    • Spoke for the first time — through the cracked, venting basin, to Ginkgo alone, after the Warden's death
    • Revealed she has been bound nearly a year and inverted — forced to eat Willowshore's good memories down the ley line
    • Named the binding rite: the Root Correction, the Second Key — and the terms of its reversal (steps in reverse, voice and action, give back the name, undo the seals; originator not required)
    • Confirmed the grove need not die — overturning Session Eleven's verdict — but that Willowshore's stolen memories are gone for good
    • Denied sending the Warden; passed Ginkgo's deception read; promised eyes and ears in the woods once freed
