    The Order of the Palatine Eye¶

    "When the town closes its hand against you, seek the eye that opens."

    Overview¶
    Type Occult investigator order (legendary, presumed extinct)
    Headquarters A site in the mountains roughly 40–50 miles south of Willowshore — exact location ingrained in Da Baishan's memory by Vujravati's gift
    Status According to common occult knowledge, wiped out; their lore is "lost to the continent, lost to the world." Whether anyone remains is unknown.

    Description¶

    The Order of the Palatine Eye is a legendary occult-investigator order — described in surviving occult studies as the "SSA or CIA of occult investigations" — a body of elite practitioners who studied, catalogued, and confronted the ancient gods, lost traditions, and forbidden practices that other groups would not touch.

    In their day they were one of the foremost authorities in the world on:

    • Ancient and "buried" gods
    • Old chthonic traditions and rites that pre-date the imperial pantheon
    • The arcane and occult techniques that other orders considered too dangerous to preserve
    • Long-running, slow corruptions of the kind Vujravati now calls "the rot spreading"

    The standard occult-history line is that the Order has been wiped out — their archives lost, their members destroyed, their lore scattered. Whether this is true, or whether some remnant persists in the mountain stronghold the party is now headed toward, is the open question.

    Da Baishan's Mark and the Path South¶

    Vujravati gave Da Baishan — alone among the party — a gift that was not a tangible object. In Session Nine, Da Baishan finally made the Occultism check (21) that revealed what the gift actually was:

    • A token tied to the Order of the Palatine Eye
    • The vision he had been carrying — "like a Google map, zoomed out from your location to a spot you know exactly" — is a fixed location in the mountains, ~40–50 miles south of Willowshore
    • The poetic phrasing he was given — "When the town closes its hand against you, seek the eye that opens" — refers to that site

    The Order is the only direct lead the party currently has on how to fight Kurosawa's chthonic geomancy. If anyone or anything remains there, they are likely the only people on the continent who would still recognize the Mother of a Thousand Wings rite by name and know how to break it.

    Why It Matters¶

    Kurosawa is a magi-grade geomancer working ritual that, by Mido's own admission, is beyond her ability to counter. The dragon Vujravati has explicitly refused to be the party's shield. The mayor has been swayed and the Council of the Magi is, in Kurosawa's own words, himself.

    The Order of the Palatine Eye is, in effect, the campaign's only present lead on counter-knowledge for what Kurosawa is doing. Whether the lead resolves into living allies, a buried archive, a guardian spirit, a trap, or a memorial is unknown.

    Open Questions¶

    • Is anyone there? Or only ruins?
    • If it is ruins — how comprehensively were they destroyed? Did anything survive in caches the way Shen Takashi's sword survived under Gosembiki?
    • Why did Vujravati direct Da Baishan to it, specifically?
    • Is the Order's silence the work of the same "rot" Vujravati witnesses, or of a separate enemy?
    • Does the Order have any connection to the five old gods Cheng Yesho named?

    Session History¶

    Session Nine (2026-05-07)¶

    • Da Baishan made the Occultism check (21) on his Vujravati gift; identified the token as a marker of the Order of the Palatine Eye
    • The vision-location was confirmed: ~40–50 miles south of Willowshore, in the mountains; the path is "ingrained in his brain"
    • Da Baishan re-interpreted the cryptic phrase "When the town closes its hand against you, seek the eye that opens" in light of the trial verdict and the chasm
    • The party voted to head south to the Order first, ahead of the Hollow of Seven Cedars, on the reasoning that any counter-knowledge they can recover there will inform every fight that follows

    Session Ten (2026-05-14)¶

    • The party reached the location ingrained in Da Baishan's Vujravati vision: a sheer cliff face in the mountains, ~40–50 miles south of Willowshore, with no door, no carving, no gate
    • On the route in, encountered the blind mountain hermit Cliché, who knew the Order without naming it ("the eye"), framed the campaign's three quest lines as a triage problem ("which wound can you bear?"), and gave them the riddle that solved the door: "Seek the cliff that has no door. Knock with the hand that doubts."
    • Da Baishan's two-knock pattern drew a closed eye in the cliff face, and the same eye, in henna-style ink, on the back of his hand. The cliff opened along the line of the eye, revealing the Palatine Eye Vault
    • Da Baishan recalled, mid-knock, that Cassian Voss had carried the same eye-mark on the back of his hand — the first hard evidence that Voss was Order, or Order-adjacent
    • Inside, the party solved the Hall of Divided Testimony — four scrolls + four dials + a heatless lantern + a panoramic mural — by reading the scrolls aloud to the lantern; the cast shadows reshaped the mural to show where each lie was wounded
    • The inner sanctum's Stone Witness rose at Boone's eye-marked approach, walked to the inscription, and opened the lectern alcove
    • Boone took the Book of Divided Testimony off the lectern. The book is blank to anyone but its keeper. Boone elected the Palatine Detective dedication — he is now the first formally inducted member of the Order in the campaign
    • Two of eight book-truths revealed themselves on first reading: Mido's Half-Truth and Kurosawa's Error
    • Da Baishan dropped his eye-tattoo on exit, ceding the bearer-mark to Boone. The cliff sealed seamlessly behind them

    Items and Doctrines Recovered (Session 10)¶

    The Book of Divided Testimony¶

    • Locked tome on a lectern in the inner sanctum; latch released to Boone's eye-mark with no keyhole. Blank to all but the keeper.
    • Catechism of the Order (first page): "Truth does not rescue. Truth is responsibility."
    • Acts as a class-style dedication for the Palatine Detective archetype. Carries fragments of testimony from past members, available as the bearer earns them. Eight possible reveals on first read; the party rolled and received two. Six remain.

    The Two Truths Revealed¶

    • Mido's Half-Truth — the elder lies by subtraction; she hides the road because of what followed the last traveler home; the tyrant believes law replaces loyalty, the geomancer believes lines replace roots
    • Kurosawa's Error — both the tyrant and the geomancer are wrong until the roots are made to forget themselves. This identifies the Hollow of Seven Cedars as the conditional joint of the entire Confluence rite

    The Order's Standing Doctrine (Cliché's framing)¶

    "Truth is not a lantern. Truth is a knife. It cuts rope, yes — and also the hand of those who uncovered too much."

    "A thing buried is not always buried by cowards. Sometimes buried by the wise."

    "When truth speaks kindly, distrust it most."
