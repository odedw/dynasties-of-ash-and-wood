    The Willowshore Ruling Family¶

    De facto rulers of a forgotten village, now facing an unwelcome change of power.

    Overview¶
    Type Noble family / Local government
    Location Willowshore (now in hidden refuges around the town rather than Silver Mist Lodge)
    Status Refused exile; gone underground; actively cooperating with the party via tunnels and stashes
    Notable Members Mido (matriarch), Cheng Yesho / The Smiling One, Kuji Yasho (son), Mido's daughter

    Description¶

    For generations, Willowshore was a forgotten place—too small and poor to warrant attention from Shogun Tsuneni's regime in Jyito. In the absence of oni rule, a local family rose to become the de facto rulers, managing the village's affairs and maintaining order.

    Now, with a new oni mayor arriving to claim authority, the family's position is threatened. They have refused to participate in the traditional Revival of Friendship—a ceremony meant to publicly put old grudges to rest and recognize the transfer of power.

    The Family's Shame¶

    The ruling family carries a secret shame connected to the oldest story about the forest shadows. The tale's ending is never spoken aloud because it embarrasses them:

    The heir to the family fortune was said to have been "lured away by forest spirits." In truth, he simply got drunk and wandered into the woods. The father—a teetotaler—spun the yarn about spirits to save face, and the family has maintained the fiction ever since.

    This deception has created factions within Willowshore. Some villagers resent the family's dishonesty; others welcomed the oni's arrival as a chance to unseat the old rulers.

    Current Situation¶

    The family's refusal to perform the Revival of Friendship is an act of quiet defiance—an unofficial protest against the oni's claim to have "always been here." But their position is precarious. They have lost their monopoly on power, and their old secrets may yet come to light.

    With Magistrate Kurosawa's arrival, the family has been displaced from their estate entirely. They are now staying at Silver Mist Lodge, a faded inn on the riverside, while the magistrate occupies their former home. This public humiliation has only deepened their resentment.

    The magistrate considered them the prime suspects for the warehouse fire that destroyed festival preparations. This suspicion proved correct: Mido, the family matriarch, admitted that "more brash" family members hired Hong to destroy the fireworks as a sabotage against the festival. She promised the party that all further planned disruptions would be canceled.

    The family is also actively surveilling Magistrate Kurosawa, having tracked his repeated visits to the Mourndusk Willow in the Dark Woods. The glen where the tree stands is connected to a tragedy in the family's past.

    Session History¶

    Session Zero (2026-01-16)¶

    • Established through world-building as Willowshore's de facto rulers
    • Their refusal to participate in the Revival of Friendship established
    • The secret of the "lost heir" and the forest story created
    • Connected to Littlefinger's backstory

    Session One (2026-01-30)¶

    • Displaced from their estate by Magistrate Kurosawa
    • Now residing at Silver Mist Lodge
    • Named by the magistrate as prime suspects for the warehouse fire
    • The party has been tasked with investigating them

    Session Two (2026-02-05)¶

    • Mido, the matriarch, formally introduced; received the party in her suite at Silver Mist Lodge
    • Admitted to sponsoring the warehouse sabotage via Hong; called it a "foolish endeavor"
    • Promised to cancel all other planned disruptions in exchange for the party's honesty
    • Honored the 100 gold payment to Hong
    • Revealed intelligence about Kurosawa's secret visits to the Mourndusk Willow
    • Warned the party about Kurosawa's arcane abilities
    • Established a cautious but cooperative relationship with the party
    • The family's past tragedy connected to the Mourndusk Willow's glen

    Session Three (2026-02-12)¶

    • Mido received Ginkgo and Donkey for a second audience; analyzed the ritual findings from the Mourndusk Willow
    • Identified the ritual as a summoning of the "Mother of a Thousand Wings"—an ancient pestilence demon
    • Revealed that Willowshore sits at a convergence of ley lines—explaining the magistrate's true interest in the village
    • Disclosed two secret entrances into the Yeshou estate (now occupied by the magistrate): stables → wine cellar, and through the koi pond
    • Acknowledged the ritual is beyond her knowledge to counter, but suggested searching the magistrate's quarters for clues
    • Mido's intelligence network continues to monitor the magistrate's movements
    • The family's cooperation with the party deepens as the threat from the magistrate becomes clearer

    Session Five (2026-03-12)¶

    • Voted to stay in Willowshore permanently after the ceremony; the family refused to abandon their ancestral home
    • Mido revealed the Gosembiki (五芒星, Five Pointed Star)—haunted ruins east of town that Kurosawa has named and recently visited
    • Cheng Yesho identified as Mido's brother and a military veteran; his Chthonic language skills translated the Gosembiki name
    • Littlefinger returned Cheng's military medal (stolen from the estate); the family was appreciative
    • Agreed to provide an alibi for the party—stating they were present for a family vote during the festival
    • Mido confirmed the pattern of fives (ley lines, lanterns, Gosembiki) and expressed belief that the spirits of the land are unhappy
    • The family's cooperation with the party is now fully established; they share intelligence and provide cover

    Session Six (2026-03-19)¶

    • Sentenced to exile in five days by Magistrate Kurosawa, who claims they stole items vital to the Imperium and the Council of the Magi; a carriage will arrive to transport them
    • Mido described the legal situation as grim—"trials of fairness" are not something the oni handle regularly
    • The family has discussed contingency plans for escape if the exile cannot be prevented
    • Cheng revealed knowledge of the old gods and the Battle of Five Crows, exchanging loaded glances with Mido before answering the party's questions
    • One of the family's two bodyguards was found dead—transformed into an infected werewolf ~8 miles east of Willowshore; the cause of his infection is unknown
    • The family continues to provide the party's alibi at personal risk, despite their own precarious situation
    • The party promised to petition Mayor Masru to intervene in the exile

    Session Nine (2026-05-07)¶

    • The family did not wait for Kurosawa's exile escort (due in three days). Mido relocated everyone out of Silver Mist Lodge into a series of hidden refuges scattered around Willowshore
    • Kuji Yasho — Mido's son — revealed and named for the first time. Led the rescue of the imprisoned party through the family's smuggling-tunnel network under the town
    • The smuggling tunnels predate the family's recent troubles by generations — Mido: "In our early ascent to the ruling family, they were used by many a Yeshou back in the day. Every once in a while they come in very handy." The network includes routes under several warehouses and the family's former estate
    • The family's surviving guard accompanied Mido at the cave outcropping west of town
    • Provisioned the party for the southern journey — full plate, healer's kits, sling, five healing potions, and information on the Hollow of Seven Cedars
    • Pledged to stay close to Willowshore and use the tunnel network and the family's spy contacts to monitor Kurosawa while the party travels to the Order of the Palatine Eye
    • The family is now fully aligned with the party in opposing Kurosawa's confluence ritual; they have moved from passive intelligence-sharing to direct operational support
