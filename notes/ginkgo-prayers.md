# Ginkgo's Farewells

*Short prayers for the fallen. Every death is a return to the cycle.*

---

## For Kin (Leshies, Plantfolk, Creatures of Root)

*Spoken with grief — these are his own.*

- "Return, kin — the mycelium remembers your true names."
- "Go back to the green dark, brother; the network will carry you home."
- "Sleep in the roots — your voice will rise again in spring leaves."
- "Kin of spore and bark, be at peace — the forest never forgets its own."
- "The garden welcomes you home."

---

## For Beasts (Wolves, Bears, Wild Things)

*Spoken gently — the oldest cycle, and the simplest.*

- "Go well into the soil — the forest will know your name by morning."
- "Rest now, old hunter; something will grow from where you fell."
- "Your hunger is fed — feed the earth in turn."
- "Return to grass and bone; you were never anyone's to keep."
- "The cycle claims you gently; may the roots be kind."

---

## For Mortals (Humans, Elves, Halflings, Dwarves, Oni)

*Spoken respectfully — shorter lives, same soil.*

- "Short life, long return — the earth keeps all its guests."
- "Walk gently into the soil; even your kind belong to the cycle."
- "You are seed now — be patient."
- "Your story ends; the story of the soil continues."
- "The bones you leave will feed what comes after."

---

## For the Corrupted (Infected Wolves, Zombie Leshies, the Warped)

*Spoken with relief — the poison ends, and the cycle resumes.*

- "The poison ends here; return to the green dark."
- "What was twisted is unmade — go back and be whole."
- "Sleep past the sickness — the cycle was always yours."
- "Released from the warp, kin — find the roots again."
- "The wrong is unwritten; return as you were meant to."

---

## For Undead and Bound Spirits

*Spoken firmly — the tether kept them too long.*

- "Your tether is cut — be still; the cycle was waiting."
- "Go where the roots can finally find you."
- "You were kept too long; the soil is patient."
- "No more hands hold you here. Rest."
- "The cycle takes back what was stolen."

---

## For Violent Enemies

*Spoken without softness, but without cruelty.*

- "Even you belong to the soil; go and feed something kinder."
- "The cycle does not judge — only receives. Go."
- "You fed on the living; now feed the green."
- "Return — what grows from you is no longer your choice."
- "Your cruelty ends; the earth is not particular."

---

## For the Young or Wrongly Taken

*Spoken quietly — the cycle keeps its promise even here.*

- "Too soon, too soon — but the roots are patient for you."
- "A small seed still makes a tree. Rest, little one."
- "The forest will wait to know your name."
- "Short sprout, long root. Go well."

---

## For Strangers and Unnamed Dead

*For old bones, battlefield dead, bodies without stories.*

- "Stranger to me, kin to the soil — welcome home."
- "I did not know your name; the earth already does."
- "Many names, one soil."
- "A long walk ends in one garden."

---

## For Things That Were Never Alive (Constructs, Stone Guardians)

*Spoken mostly out of habit — no cycle to return to, but a farewell is given.*

- "Stone to stone — no prayer is owed, but one is given."
- "You were borrowed shape; be borrowed again."
- "Dust, and nothing more — but the dust will grow something."

---

## All-Purpose (When in Doubt)

- "The soil welcomes you."
- "By tomorrow the forest will know your name."
- "Decay is only another spring."
- "Be at peace — the cycle holds."
- "Return."
